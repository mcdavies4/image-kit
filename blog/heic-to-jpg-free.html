How to Convert HEIC to JPG Free — iPhone Photos on Any Device

HEIC is Apple's default photo format since iOS 11. It produces smaller files than JPG at the same quality — which is why Apple uses it. The problem: Windows, Android and many web services don't support HEIC natively. You take a photo on your iPhone, transfer it to your laptop, and Windows Photo Viewer can't open it.

Why does my iPhone save photos as HEIC?

Apple introduced HEIC (High Efficiency Image Container) in iOS 11 to save storage space. A HEIC photo is typically half the size of an equivalent JPG with no visible quality loss. Great for your iPhone's storage — less great for compatibility with the rest of the world.

How to stop iPhone saving as HEIC

If you want your iPhone to always save as JPG: Settings → Camera → Formats → Most Compatible. This switches from HEIC to JPG going forward. Existing HEIC photos stay as HEIC.
